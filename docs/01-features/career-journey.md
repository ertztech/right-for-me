# Career Journey

## Purpose

Define the desired end-to-end user experience for how someone enters NextMove, uncovers their professional story, and moves toward an opportunity decision with confidence.

## Key Decisions

- Interview-first onboarding should help the user talk through their story instead of filling out cold forms.
- The experience should be chapter-based so progress feels intentional and easier to resume.
- Motion is a reward for progress, not decoration.
- Career Brain is the reusable source of truth behind the experience.
- The first-time experience should feel memorable, guided, and high-confidence.
- The returning experience should be fast, calm, and non-distracting.
- AI should uncover stories, patterns, and evidence instead of forcing the user to manually invent structured answers.

## Experience Shape

### First-Time User

The product should feel like a guided conversation that helps someone surface career evidence they already have but may not know how to articulate. The system should turn those stories into reusable structured material without losing warmth.

### Returning User

The product should quickly route the user back to the most relevant next action, opportunity, or chapter without replaying onboarding theatrics.

## Implications For Planning

- Features should preserve a distinction between memorable onboarding and efficient return visits.
- Career Brain updates should benefit every later workflow.
- UX and content choices should support confidence-building, not novelty for its own sake.
