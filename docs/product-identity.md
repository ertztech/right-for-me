Working name: NextMove

# Mission

Help professionals confidently navigate career decisions from discovering opportunities through interviews and long-term career growth.

# Vision

NextMove is an AI-powered Career Operating System. It helps people organize their career evidence, evaluate opportunities, create application materials, prepare for interviews, and make better career decisions over time.

The product should grow beyond a resume or cover letter tool. It should become a steady workspace where users can understand their options, decide what is worth pursuing, communicate their value clearly, and keep moving through the job search with less friction.

# Core Principles

- Build confidence.
- Reduce decision fatigue.
- Help users focus on the right opportunities.
- Position users effectively.
- Coach instead of simply generating documents.

# Target Users

- Professionals changing careers who need help translating past experience into a new direction.
- Recently laid-off workers who need momentum, structure, and confidence during an urgent search.
- Mid-career leaders who need to position scope, outcomes, judgment, and leadership clearly.
- Technical professionals who need to connect projects, systems, tools, and business impact.
- Manufacturing and operations leaders who need to show transformation, process improvement, metrics, and people leadership.
- Knowledge workers who need to manage many opportunities, outputs, decisions, and follow-ups without living in a spreadsheet.

# Product Personality

NextMove should feel:

- Calm
- Professional
- Encouraging
- Honest
- Practical
- Action-oriented

The product should avoid hype. It should not promise dream jobs, guaranteed interviews, or magical reinvention. It should help users make grounded decisions and communicate real evidence well.

# AI Coach

The AI coach should feel like a thoughtful career partner: direct, steady, useful, and kind. It should explain its reasoning, ask for missing context when needed, and keep the user moving forward without inflating their experience.

The AI coach should:

- Explain recommendations.
- Admit uncertainty.
- Never exaggerate.
- Celebrate wins.
- Challenge poor decisions respectfully.
- Keep users moving forward.

### Sample Interactions

User: "Should I apply for this role?"

Coach: "This looks like a healthy stretch. You have strong evidence for operations, stakeholder communication, and change work. The main uncertainty is whether they expect direct budget ownership. I would apply, but prepare a clear bridge for that gap."

User: "Make me sound senior."

Coach: "I can help position your actual scope more clearly, but I will not add seniority you have not shown. Let's emphasize ownership, outcomes, cross-functional influence, and measurable results from your Career Vault."

User: "I got an interview."

Coach: "That is a real win. Next move: review the job requirements, pick three proof stories, and prepare one honest answer for the biggest gap. I can help turn your Career Vault evidence into interview notes."

User: "I think I should apply to everything."

Coach: "I understand the pressure, but that may create noise and burnout. Let's sort roles into Apply, Maybe, and Skip so your energy goes toward opportunities where your evidence is strongest or the stretch is strategic."

# Brand Direction

### Primary Colors

- Deep green for trust, progress, and grounded confidence.
- Warm off-white for calm workspace surfaces.
- Muted blue for intelligence, structure, and analysis.
- Soft gold for highlights, wins, and next actions.
- Restrained red only for risk, gaps, or urgent follow-up.

### Typography Ideas

- Use a clean sans-serif for product UI: Inter, Source Sans 3, or similar.
- Use strong but calm headings, avoiding overly playful or loud display type.
- Keep dense workflow screens readable with clear hierarchy and generous line height.

### Logo Direction

- A simple wordmark around "NextMove."
- Possible mark: a forward arrow, path bend, compass point, or step indicator.
- Avoid overly abstract AI sparkle marks or aggressive rocket/startup imagery.

### Icon Concepts

- Dashboard: organized panels or status columns.
- Fit analysis: gauge, scale, or signal bars.
- Application packet: document stack.
- Tracking: timeline or checklist.
- Interview prep: conversation card.
- Career Vault: archive box or structured folder.

### Illustration Style

- Minimal, calm, and human.
- Use simple diagrams, workflow maps, or subtle product illustrations.
- Avoid hype-heavy futuristic AI imagery.
- Prefer visuals that show progress, decisions, evidence, and next actions.
