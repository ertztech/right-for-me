# Backlog Review Workflow

Purpose:
Keep the backlog clean, prioritized, and small enough that the next important work is visible.

Reference example:
- ../examples/backlog-review-example.md

Cycle boundary:
- Implementation Cycle uses Backlog Review after Parking Lot Review to prioritize candidate work for the Cycle Backlog.

Referenced roles:
- ../agents/facilitator-orchestrator.md
- ../agents/product-owner.md
- ../agents/engineering-manager.md
- ../agents/delivery-coach.md
- ../product-direction.md

When to use:
- New ideas have piled up.
- Priorities feel stale or fuzzy.
- Too much work is sitting in proposed or blocked status.
- The founder needs to decide what deserves refinement next.

Process:
1. Facilitator frames the review using product direction.
2. Product Owner groups items by value, urgency, clarity, and readiness.
3. Engineering Manager flags sequencing or dependency concerns.
4. Delivery Coach flags items likely to create large or messy implementation packages.
5. Facilitator surfaces disagreements and recommends what moves forward.

Rules:
- Keep the backlog intentionally small.
- Promote only work that has a clear reason to exist now.
- Push unclear work back to refinement instead of pretending it is ready.
- Move low-value noise to archive or discard.
- Do not execute Trello changes automatically.

Required Output:
1. Priority order
2. Promote now
3. Refine next
4. Keep parked
5. Archive
6. Discard
7. Agreements
8. Disagreements
9. Assumptions
10. Confidence
11. Decision-required items
