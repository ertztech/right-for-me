# Product Direction

Purpose:
Keep planning grounded in why NextMove exists and what matters right now.

## Non-Negotiable Principles

- NextMove helps people choose the right opportunity and improve their odds of getting an interview.
- AI informs judgment. The user owns the final application decision.
- Honest confidence is better than inflated confidence.
- Career Journey should encourage imperfect progress rather than demand polished answers.
- Keep the product lightweight, practical, and explainable.
- Add framework complexity only when real work exposes a planning gap.
- Prefer focused workflow improvements over broad platform expansion.
- Product changes must be documentable, reviewable, and small enough to ship safely.

## Strategic Goals

- Help users assess fit, stretch, and likely positioning faster.
- Turn messy job-search inputs into clear next actions.
- Make strengths, risks, and evidence visible enough to support better choices.
- Support repeatable product and delivery decisions without burying humans in process.

## Current Objectives

- Use Implementation Cycle as the preferred delivery container.
- Keep the cycle normally to one day and at most two days.
- Strengthen planning before Codex implementation starts.
- Produce clearer ready stories with visible tradeoffs and confidence.
- Keep the backlog clean enough that approved work can move into GitHub with minimal churn.
- Use Trello as the human-approved product intent layer, not as autonomous execution.

## Delivery Model

- Implementation Cycle is the preferred delivery container.
- The cycle normally lasts one day.
- The cycle may extend to two days.
- Implementation Planning prepares one implementation.
- Multiple implementations may occur within one cycle.
- Each implementation retains its own branch, pull request, review, and merge decision.
- The Cycle Backlog is a forecast, not a commitment.

## Decision Filters

Ask these questions before recommending work:

1. Does this improve job-fit, stretch, or application-positioning decisions?
2. Does this clarify the next best action for the user or the team?
3. Is this small enough to explain, test, and review in one feature-sized change?
4. Are we solving a real current problem rather than adding speculative process?
5. Can the human decision maker see the tradeoffs clearly?

## Success Metrics

- More backlog items reach ready status without major rework.
- Implementation Planning outputs contain explicit risks, assumptions, and decision points.
- Codex receives smaller, clearer approved prompts.
- Product documentation quality improves enough that implementation ambiguity drops.
- Trello usage stays human-controlled and auditable.

## Known Unknowns

- Which planning artifacts will prove most useful over repeated cycles.
- How much disagreement visibility the team wants in day-to-day use.
- What sizing patterns best predict effort for this repo over time.
- When backlog refinement should stop and deeper product discovery should start.
- Which Trello workflows will stay manual versus receive approved tooling support later.
